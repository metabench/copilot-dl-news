Council Gives Green Light to Riverside Park Expansion

By Tom Wilson, Staff Writer | Dec 18, 2025

SMALLTOWN - The city council voted 5-2 Tuesday night to approve a $2.3 million expansion of Riverside Park, marking the largest parks investment in the city's history.

The project will add a new playground, walking trails, and a community garden to the popular park on the west side of town.

"This is a great day for our community," said Mayor Sarah Johnson after the vote. "Families have been asking for improved park facilities for years."

Construction is expected to begin in March and be completed by fall. The project will be funded through a combination of city bonds and a state recreation grant.

Councilmember Bob Davis and Helen Chen voted against the measure, citing concerns about the impact on traffic in the surrounding neighborhood.

The expansion will include:

  • New playground with accessible equipment
  • 1.5 miles of paved walking trails
  • Community garden with 40 plots
  • Improved parking with 50 additional spaces

Residents can view the complete project plans at City Hall or on the city website.
