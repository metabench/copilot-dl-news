Top 10 Programming Languages to Learn in 2026

Stay ahead of the curve with these in-demand skills

By Jennifer Lee December 18, 2025 12 min read

The programming landscape continues to evolve rapidly. Whether you're a seasoned developer or just starting out, knowing which languages are in demand can shape your career trajectory. Here are the top 10 programming languages you should consider learning in 2026.

10. Rust

Rust continues to gain momentum for systems programming, offering memory safety without garbage collection. Major companies including Microsoft and Google are increasingly adopting it for performance-critical applications.

9. Go

Google's Go language remains essential for cloud infrastructure and microservices. Its simplicity and excellent concurrency support make it ideal for distributed systems.

8. Kotlin

Now Google's preferred language for Android development, Kotlin also works well for server-side applications with its JVM compatibility.

7. Swift

Apple's Swift continues to mature for iOS, macOS, and even server-side development, with growing adoption in enterprise mobile projects.

6. TypeScript

TypeScript has become the standard for large-scale JavaScript applications, with nearly all major frameworks now offering first-class TypeScript support.
